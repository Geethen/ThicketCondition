Intact: dense, continuous woody-thicket canopy.
Moderate: opened or patchy canopy, but substantial thicket remains.
Severe: sparse remnants, extensive bare or eroded ground.
Transformed: thicket has been replaced by a human land use such as cultivation, plantation, settlement, road or quarry.
No thicket: naturally non-thicket habitat or a point outside the target ecosystem; do not infer transformation without evidence.

Judge the point centre. Compare dates when shadows, fire, seasonality or land-use history make the class unclear. Use Unsure and add a reason when the imagery cannot support a defensible decision.
