# Instructions for thicket-condition labellers

The coordinator will send you one personal assignment link. It looks like:

`https://geethen.github.io/ThicketCondition/?assignment=GS`

Use that exact link every time. Your assignment code is not a password, but it
selects your point list, so do not remove or change the `?assignment=...` part.

## Before starting

1. Open your personal link in a normal browser window, not private/incognito mode.
2. Check the blue assignment box. It must show the campaign, your assignment
   code, and the number of points you were given. If it says “Coordinator mode”
   or reports an unknown assignment, stop and contact the coordinator.
3. Enter your name or initials. Use the same name throughout the campaign.

## Labelling

1. Read the field guidance at the top before beginning.
2. Judge the centre of the sample point using more than one imagery source or
   Wayback date when the interpretation is unclear.
3. Choose one class. Use **Unsure**, a note, and a review flag when the imagery
   does not support a defensible decision; do not guess.
4. Your progress is saved automatically in this browser. Prefer to continue on
   the same device and browser.

Some points are intentionally assigned to two people. This is blind QA, not a
mistake. Do not compare labels with another labeller until the coordinator has
closed the campaign.

## Backups and submission

1. At the end of every work session, select **Review & download**.
2. Choose **JSON (full fidelity)** and download a backup, even if the assignment
   is incomplete.
3. Keep your latest backup. To resume on another device, open your same personal
   link and use **Upload / resume**.
4. When finished, download the final JSON and send it to the coordinator using
   the agreed file-sharing method. Do not rename the file unless asked.

The app is not connected to a central server. Closing the browser does not send
your work to the coordinator; the downloaded final file is your submission.
